Overview

There are some tutorials, such as Multimodal Machine Learning tutorial in CVPR 2016 and Recent Vision-and-Language Tutorial in CVPR 2020, which introduce works in multimodal learning and its applications. However, most of works focus on visual and text modalities and none of recent existing tutorials cover advances in audio-visual learning. There are indeed some workshops (e.g., sight and sound and multimodal learning) that includes audio-visual papers. However, they are not for educational purpose. Recently, many new audio- visual learning problems and applications appeared. As an emerging new field, it has already attracted a lot of research interest, for example, WACV awarded the best student paper to an audio-visual learning work last year. We believe that it is necessary to have an audio-visual learning tutorial to introduce recent advances in audio-visual learning and advocate more people to explore the new research filed.
